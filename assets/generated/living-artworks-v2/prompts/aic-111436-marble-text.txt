Photorealistic walkable impossible still-life chamber inspired by the spatial tensions of a late-nineteenth-century apple still life, rendered as a physically built museum set rather than a painting. The hero composition occupies a shallow stage immediately in front of the viewer: two visibly disjoint tilted wood table edges follow conflicting vanishing points; a forward-pitching worn wicker basket overflows with irregular heirloom apples; a tall leaning dark-green aged glass bottle divides the composition; a glazed ceramic plate holds crusty bread; and heavy striped white linen cascades toward the viewer in sculptural folds. Leave one natural apple-sized depression empty in the front-center linen for a separately animated hero object. Preserve this intentionally impossible geometry instead of correcting the tables into a normal rectangle. Surround the stage with a restrained blue-green lime-plaster chamber that feels plausible but secondary. Museum-grade tactile realism: individual wicker strands, matte wax bloom and russet bruises on fruit, subsurface fruit skin, dust and micro-scratches in glass, ceramic glaze, bread pores, scuffed wood grain, thick woven linen fibers, realistic cloth weight, ambient occlusion and precise contact shadows. Soft north-window daylight from upper left, neutral filmic exposure, deep focus, natural color separation. Absolutely no brush strokes, canvas texture, illustration, painterly shading, cartoon look, plastic fruit, glossy supermarket apples, people, labels, text, frames, oversized furniture, duplicated baskets, or decorative props. The impossible tabletop must be the immediate focal environment and fill the central field of view.
